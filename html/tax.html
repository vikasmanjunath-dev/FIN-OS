First Principles: What is Tax?

Tax is not theft. Tax is the Subscription Fee for living in India.

Just like you pay Netflix to watch movies, you pay the Government to access:

  • 🛣️ Infrastructure — Roads, Bridges, Metro, Railways
  • 🛡️ Defence — Army, Navy, Air Force, Border Security
  • 🏥 Healthcare — AIIMS, Government Hospitals, Ayushman Bharat
  • 🎓 Education — IITs, IIMs, Public Schools, Mid-day Meals
  • ⚖️ Law & Order — Police, Courts, Justice System
  • 💰 Welfare — PM-KISAN, MGNREGA, Subsidies
🇮🇳

Desi Reality: You don't pay tax because you earn money. You pay tax because you participate in the economy. Even a beggar buying a ₹5 Parle-G pays tax (GST). Only ~7% of Indians pay Income Tax directly — but 100% pay Indirect Taxes.

The Structure: Two Engines of Taxation

The Indian Tax System has two fundamental engines. Every tax you'll ever pay falls into one of these.

🎯

1. Direct Taxes

"The Slap on the Face"

Paid directly by you to the government. It hurts more because you see the money leaving your bank account.

  • Income Tax — On Salary, Business, Profession
  • Capital Gains Tax — On Profit from selling Stocks, Property, Gold
  • Corporate Tax — On Company Profits (22-25%)
  • Securities Transaction Tax (STT) — On Stock Market Trades
  • Dividend Distribution Tax — Abolished, now taxed in your hands

Controlled by: CBDT (Central Board of Direct Taxes) under Ministry of Finance

🍔

2. Indirect Taxes

"The Silent Pinch"

Collected from you, but paid by the seller. You pay this every time you consume something. Hidden in the price.

  • GST — Goods & Services Tax (Replaced VAT, Service Tax, Excise)
  • Customs Duty — On Imported Goods (Why iPhones are expensive)
  • Excise Duty — On Petroleum & Liquor (State revenue)
  • Stamp Duty — On Property Registration (5-7%)
  • Road Tax / Toll — On Vehicle Registration & Highway Usage

Controlled by: CBIC (Central Board of Indirect Taxes & Customs)

PART 2 INCOME TAX DEEP DIVE
04

Income Tax: The 5 Heads

The government doesn't just see "Money". It classifies your income into 5 specific buckets. Your income must fit into one of these.

💼

1. Salary

Basic Pay, HRA, LTA, Bonus, Gratuity, Leave Encashment. The main source for most salaried Indians.

🏠

2. House Property

Rental income from property you own. Even if the house is empty, "deemed rent" may apply.

🏭

3. PGBP

Profits & Gains from Business or Profession — Freelancers, Shopkeepers, Consultants, F&O Traders.

📈

4. Capital Gains

Profit from selling capital assets — Stocks, Mutual Funds, Property, Gold, Bonds, Crypto.

🎁

5. Other Sources

FD Interest, Dividends, Lottery Winnings, Gifts > ₹50k, Family Pension, Savings Account Interest.

🧠 Deep Dive: Salary Income Breakdown

For most Indians, salary is the primary income source. Understanding its components helps you optimize tax.

Allowances
  • HRA — House Rent Allowance (Partly exempt if you pay rent)
  • LTA — Leave Travel Allowance (Exempt for domestic travel, 2x in 4 years)
  • DA — Dearness Allowance (Inflation adjustment)
  • Special Allowance — Fully taxable
Perquisites (Taxable)
  • Company Car for personal use
  • ESOPs (Taxed when exercised + when sold)
  • Rent-free accommodation
  • Club memberships paid by employer
  • Interest-free loans from employer
Deductions from Salary
  • Standard Deduction — Flat ₹75,000 (FY 2025-26)
  • Professional Tax — Max ₹2,500/year (state-level)
  • Entertainment Allowance — Only for Govt employees
05

Tax Slabs: Old vs New Regime (FY 2025-26)

India gives you a choice — pick the regime that saves you more tax. The New Regime is the default from FY 2023-24.

💡

Which should you pick? If you claim HRA, have a home loan, invest ₹1.5L in 80C, and pay health insurance — Old Regime likely saves more. If you have no deductions or investments, New Regime's lower slabs win. Calculate both before filing.

06

Surcharge & Cess: The Tax on Tax

Your tax liability doesn't end at slab rates. The government adds two extra layers.

📊

Surcharge

A "super tax" on high earners, calculated as a percentage of your income tax.

  • Income ₹50L–₹1Cr → 10% surcharge
  • Income ₹1Cr–₹2Cr → 15% surcharge
  • Income ₹2Cr–₹5Cr → 25% surcharge
  • Income > ₹5Cr → 37% surcharge (Old) / 25% (New, capped)
🏥

Health & Education Cess

A flat 4% charged on (Income Tax + Surcharge). Goes to healthcare and education funds.

Income Tax (Example): ₹15,00,000
Surcharge (10%): ₹1,50,000
Cess (4% on Tax+SC): ₹66,000
Total Tax: ₹17,16,000
07

Section 80: The Tax Shields

These deductions reduce your taxable income. Only applicable in the Old Regime (except 80CCD(2) for employer NPS contribution).

🛡️

Section 80C

Limit: ₹1.5 Lakh
  • EPF / PPF (Retirement Savings)
  • ELSS Mutual Funds (3-year lock-in)
  • LIC Premium Payments
  • Home Loan Principal Repayment
  • NSC (National Savings Certificates)
  • Sukanya Samriddhi (Daughter's account)
  • 5-Year Tax-Saving FD
  • Tuition Fees (Max 2 children)
🏥

Section 80D

Limit: ₹25k – ₹1L
  • Self & Family: ₹25,000
  • Parents (below 60): ₹25,000
  • Parents (above 60): ₹50,000
  • Preventive Health Check: ₹5,000 (within limit)
  • Max: ₹1,00,000 (if both self & parents are senior citizens)
🎓

Section 80E

No Upper Limit

Interest on Education Loan — for self, spouse, or children. Available for 8 years from the year of first repayment. Only interest, not principal.

🤝

Section 80G

50% or 100%

Donations to approved charities. PM Relief Fund & CM Relief Fund get 100% deduction. Others get 50%. Cash donations > ₹2,000 not allowed.

👴

Section 80CCD

Extra ₹50,000
  • 80CCD(1): NPS contribution (within 80C limit)
  • 80CCD(1B): Additional ₹50,000 for NPS (above 80C)
  • 80CCD(2): Employer NPS contribution (14% of salary, no limit) — Available in BOTH regimes!
🏠

Section 24(b)

Limit: ₹2 Lakh

Home Loan Interest deduction. Self-occupied property: ₹2L max. Let-out property: No limit on interest deduction. This is separate from 80C principal.

🧮

Section 10 Exemptions (separate from 80C): HRA Exemption, LTA (Actual domestic travel cost exempt), Gratuity (₹20L exempt), Leave Encashment (₹25L on retirement), Children Education Allowance (₹100/month/child), Agricultural Income (fully exempt).

09

Equity & Stock Market Taxation

If you trade or invest in stocks, you're dealing with multiple taxes simultaneously.

📊

Securities Transaction Tax (STT)

A tax collected at the time of every transaction — not on profit, but on turnover.

  • Equity Delivery Buy: 0.1% of value
  • Equity Delivery Sell: 0.1% of value
  • Intraday Sell: 0.025% of value
  • Futures Sell: 0.0125% of value
  • Options Sell (on exercise): 0.0625% of value

STT is automatically deducted by your broker. It's not deductible from capital gains but shows in your contract notes.

💰

Dividend Taxation

Since April 2020, dividends are taxed in your hands at your slab rate.

  • Added to your total income → taxed at your slab rate
  • TDS of 10% if dividend > ₹5,000/year from a company
  • Section 80M: Deduction for inter-corporate dividends
  • Expense deduction: Interest on loan taken to buy shares (max 20% of dividend)

Example: You receive ₹80,000 dividend. It gets added to your salary income and taxed at your slab (say 30%). Tax = ₹24,000. TDS of ₹8,000 already cut → you pay ₹16,000 more.

📉 Intraday & Short-Term Trading

Intraday Trading

  • Classified as Speculative Business Income
  • Taxed at your slab rate
  • Speculative losses can ONLY offset speculative profits
  • Losses can be carried forward for 4 years
  • No audit required just for intraday

Delivery-Based (Short Term)

  • Held < 12 months = Short Term Capital Gain
  • Taxed at flat 20%
  • STCG losses can offset ANY capital gains (short or long term)
  • Losses can be carried forward for 8 years
  • Must file ITR to carry forward losses
10

Mutual Fund Taxation: The Complete Guide

Mutual funds are taxed based on their equity allocation, not their name.

📈

Equity MFs (>65% equity)

Index Funds, Large Cap, Small Cap, ELSS

  • STCG (< 1 year): 20%
  • LTCG (> 1 year): 12.5% above ₹1.25L
  • SIP each installment has its own holding period
  • ELSS: 3-year lock-in, but qualifies for 80C
🏦

Debt MFs (<35% equity)

Liquid, Gilt, Corporate Bond, Money Market

  • Purchased after Apr 2023: Always slab rate
  • No LTCG benefit anymore
  • No indexation benefit
  • Treated as "Other Sources" income
⚖️

Hybrid / Others (35-65% equity)

Balanced Advantage, Multi-Asset, Gold ETF

  • STCG (< 24 months): Slab rate
  • LTCG (> 24 months): 12.5% without indexation
  • Holding period: 24 months for LTCG
  • Gold ETFs follow this category
⚠️

SIP Tax Trap: Each SIP installment is a separate purchase. If you invest ₹10,000/month for 2 years and redeem all at once, only the first 12 installments qualify as LTCG. The rest are STCG. Plan your redemptions carefully!

11

Real Estate Taxation

Property transactions are heavily taxed — but there are powerful exemptions if you know the rules.

Buying Property

  • 💰 Stamp Duty — 5-7% of property value (varies by state)
  • 📋 Registration Charges — 1% additional
  • 🏠 GST — 5% on under-construction (no ITC), 1% for affordable housing
  • 📊 TDS — 1% if property value > ₹50 Lakhs (buyer deducts)

Selling Property

  • 📅 Held < 2 years → STCG at slab rate
  • 📅 Held > 2 years → LTCG at 12.5% (without indexation from FY24-25)

Exemptions (Save LTCG Tax)

Section 54

Reinvest LTCG in a new residential house within 2 years (purchase) or 3 years (construction). Entire LTCG is exempt.

Section 54EC

Invest LTCG in specified bonds (NHAI, REC) within 6 months. Max ₹50 Lakhs. 5-year lock-in.

Section 54F

For non-residential assets (gold, stocks). Reinvest full sale proceeds (not just LTCG) in a residential house for full exemption.

12

Gold Taxation: Every Form

Gold is India's favorite asset — but taxation varies wildly based on how you hold it.

🪙 Physical Gold / Gold ETF / Gold MF

  • Holding period for LTCG: 24 months
  • STCG (< 24 months): Slab rate
  • LTCG (> 24 months): 12.5% without indexation
  • Wealth tax: Abolished since 2015
  • Making charges: Not tax deductible
  • Customs duty: 6% on imported gold

🏛️ Sovereign Gold Bonds (SGBs)

  • Interest: 2.5% p.a. — taxed at slab rate
  • LTCG on maturity (8 years): FULLY TAX FREE 🎉
  • Early redemption (after 5 years): 12.5% LTCG
  • Sold on exchange before maturity: Capital gains apply
  • Best tax-efficient way to hold gold in India

Pro Tip: SGBs give you 2.5% interest + gold price appreciation. On maturity, capital gains are ZERO tax. This is the only asset in India with completely tax-free capital gains.

13

Crypto & F&O: The "Sin" Taxes

The government treats these as high-risk activities and taxes them aggressively.

₿

Crypto / Virtual Digital Assets (VDA)

FLAT 30% TAX — THE HARSHEST IN THE WORLD

  • Flat 30% on ALL profits — no slab benefit
  • No distinction between short-term and long-term
  • Cannot set off losses (Bitcoin loss cannot reduce Ethereum profit)
  • Cannot set off crypto losses against ANY other income
  • 1% TDS on every transaction (Section 194S)
  • No deduction allowed except cost of acquisition
  • Gifts of crypto > ₹50,000 are taxable for the receiver
  • Mining income: Taxed at 30% on fair market value
📉

F&O (Futures & Options) Trading

TREATED AS BUSINESS INCOME

  • Classified as Non-Speculative Business Income
  • Taxed at your slab rate (can be up to 30%+)
  • Losses can offset other business income
  • Losses carried forward for 8 years
  • Tax Audit mandatory if:
    • Turnover > ₹10 Crore (digital transactions)
    • Declaring losses (even ₹1 loss)
    • Profit < 6% of turnover & income > basic exemption
  • STT paid is NOT deductible from profits
  • Turnover calculation: Absolute sum of all P&L per trade

Freelancers & Business Owners

If you earn outside of a salary — consulting, freelancing, running a business — this section is for you.

Presumptive Taxation

The government knows freelancers hate accounting. So they gave a "Cheat Code":

Section 44ADA — Professionals

Doctors, Lawyers, Architects, Engineers, Accountants, Consultants, Content Creators earning up to ₹75 Lakhs can declare 50% as profit. No expense books needed.

Section 44AD — Small Business

Businesses with turnover up to ₹3 Crores (if 95% digital) can declare 8% as profit (6% for digital receipts). No audit.

⚠️

Trap: If you opt for presumptive and declare LOWER than 50%/8%, you MUST get a tax audit done. And you cannot use presumptive for next 5 years.

Freelance Revenue: ₹20,00,000
Presumed Expense (50%): - ₹10,00,000
Taxable Income: ₹10,00,000

Business Owner (Digital)

Business Revenue: ₹50,00,000
Presumed Expense (94%): - ₹47,00,000
Taxable Income: ₹3,00,000

GST: The Unified Indirect Tax

Introduced in 2017, GST replaced 17 different taxes with one. Every item/service you buy has GST embedded.

Total Price: ₹118
SGST (9%) — To State Govt
CGST (9%) — To Central Govt
Base Value: ₹100

The 4 GST Slabs

  • 0% Essentials — Milk, Bread, Fresh Vegetables, Grains, Salt
  • 5% Common — Oil, Sugar, Tea, Coffee, Coal, Medicines, Economy flight
  • 12-18% Standard — Electronics, Clothing > ₹1000, Services, Business class flight, Restaurant food
  • 28% Luxury + Sin — Cars, ACs, Tobacco, Aerated drinks, Cement, Movie tickets > ₹100

GST Types

  • CGST — Central GST (goes to Centre)
  • SGST — State GST (goes to State)
  • IGST — Integrated GST (inter-state, distributed later)
  • UTGST — Union Territory GST
🧾

GST Registration: Mandatory if annual turnover exceeds ₹40 Lakhs (goods) or ₹20 Lakhs (services). Composition scheme available for small businesses (turnover < ₹1.5 Crore) — pay flat 1-5% GST, no ITC claim.

PART 5 COMPLIANCE & FILING

TDS & Advance Tax

The government doesn't wait for you to pay. It collects tax before you even see the money.

TDS (Tax Deducted at Source)

Your employer, bank, or client cuts tax before paying you.

  • Salary — Employer deducts based on declared investments
  • FD Interest — 10% TDS if interest > ₹40,000 (₹50,000 for seniors)
  • Rent — 10% TDS if rent > ₹50,000/month
  • Freelance Payment — 10% TDS under Section 194J
  • Property Sale — 1% TDS if value > ₹50 Lakhs
  • Crypto — 1% TDS on every transaction
  • Dividend — 10% TDS if > ₹5,000/year

Check your TDS: Log into incometax.gov.in → View Form 26AS or Annual Information Statement (AIS). All TDS credits appear there.

Advance Tax (Quarterly EMI of Tax)

If your annual tax liability exceeds ₹10,000, you must pay tax in installments — don't wait till March.

15 June — 15% of annual tax 15 Sept — 45% cumulative 15 Dec — 75% cumulative 15 Mar — 100% cumulative
Penalty for Non-Payment
  • Section 234B: 1% per month interest if advance tax paid < 90%
  • Section 234C: 1% per month for each quarter of deferment
  • Senior citizens without business income: Exempt from advance tax
17

ITR Filing: Forms, Deadlines & Process

Filing your Income Tax Return (ITR) is mandatory, even if you owe zero tax in many cases.

Which ITR Form?

  • ITR-1 (Sahaj) — Salary + 1 House + Other Sources. Income up to ₹50L. Most common.
  • ITR-2 — Salary + Capital Gains + Multiple Properties + Foreign Assets. No business.
  • ITR-3 — Business/Profession income (includes F&O traders).
  • ITR-4 (Sugam) — Presumptive income under 44AD/44ADA.
  • ITR-5 — Firms, LLPs, AOPs.
  • ITR-6 — Companies (except Section 11).
  • ITR-7 — Trusts, Political parties.

Key Deadlines

31 July — ITR filing for individuals (non-audit) 31 Oct — ITR filing for audit cases 31 Dec — Belated / Revised return deadline 31 March — Updated return (ITR-U) with extra tax

Important Forms & Documents

  • Form 16 — Salary TDS certificate from employer
  • Form 26AS — All TDS/TCS credits consolidated
  • AIS — Annual Information Statement (all financial transactions)
  • TIS — Taxpayer Information Summary
  • Form 16A — TDS on non-salary income (FD, rent)

🚨 Why You MUST File ITR (Even with Zero Tax)

  • ✅ Mandatory if income > ₹2.5L (Old) / ₹3L (New)
  • ✅ Required for Visa applications (US, UK, Schengen)
  • ✅ Required for Loan approvals (Home, Car, Personal)
  • ✅ To claim TDS refunds (excess tax deducted)
  • ✅ To carry forward capital losses (stock market losses)
  • ✅ Required for credit card applications > ₹5L limit
  • ✅ Proof of income for insurance claims
  • ✅ High-value transactions trigger scrutiny if no ITR filed
18

Penalties & Consequences

The IT Department doesn't play nice with defaulters. Here's what you risk.

Late Filing

Income > ₹5L: ₹5,000 penalty. Income < ₹5L: ₹1,000. After 31 Dec: Cannot file (only ITR-U with extra tax).

Non-Filing

Up to ₹10,000 penalty + prosecution. Cannot carry forward losses. May receive IT notice under Section 142(1).

Under-Reporting

50% of tax on under-reported income. If misreporting (fraud): 200% penalty.

Tax Evasion

Criminal prosecution. 3-7 years imprisonment for willful evasion. Plus 100-300% of tax as penalty.

Cash Transactions

Cash payment > ₹10,000 to one person per day: 100% penalty. Cash > ₹2L for property: Penalized.

Interest on Late Tax

Section 234A/B/C: 1% per month simple interest on unpaid tax from due date.

NRI Taxation Basics

If you're a Non-Resident Indian, your tax obligations depend on your residential status and where your income originates.

Who is an NRI?

  • Stayed in India < 182 days in a financial year
  • Or stayed < 60 days in the current year AND < 365 days in preceding 4 years
  • Indian citizens going abroad for employment: 182-day rule applies (60-day rule doesn't)

What's Taxable for NRIs?

  • Only Indian-sourced income is taxable
  • Salary received in India: Taxable
  • Rent from Indian property: Taxable (30% TDS)
  • Capital gains from Indian assets: Taxable
  • Interest on NRO account: Taxable
  • Interest on NRE/FCNR account: Tax-free
  • Foreign income: NOT taxable in India
  • DTAA (Double Taxation Avoidance) prevents paying tax twice

The "Hidden" Taxes You're Already Paying

Beyond income tax and GST, there are taxes embedded everywhere in your daily life.

Property Tax

Paid annually to Municipality. Based on property area, location & type. Ranges from ₹2,000 to ₹50,000+.

Stamp Duty

5-7% of property value paid at registration. Maharashtra: 6%, Karnataka: 5%, Delhi: 6%. A HUGE hidden cost.

Customs Duty

10-100% on imports. Why iPhones cost more. Gold: 6%. Electronics: 10-20%. Luxury goods: up to 100%.

Professional Tax

State-level, deducted from salary. Max ₹2,500/year. Maharashtra, Karnataka charge it. Some states don't.

Fuel Taxes

Petrol price: ~50% is tax (Excise + VAT). ₹100 petrol = ₹50 tax. Varies by state. Not under GST.

TCS (Tax Collected at Source)

5% TCS on foreign remittance > ₹7L (travel, education). 20% on non-education foreign spending > ₹7L.

Angel Tax

Tax on startup funding received above "fair market value". Relaxed for DPIIT-recognized startups.

Equalisation Levy

2% "Google Tax" on foreign digital companies earning from India. Paid by them but passed to you as cost.

Tax-Saving Strategies (Legal)

Smart tax planning can save you lakhs every year. Here are the strategies the pros use.

For Salaried Individuals

  • 🏠 Restructure Salary — Ask employer to increase HRA, add food coupons (₹26,400 exempt), NPS contribution
  • 💰 Max 80C — EPF + PPF + ELSS = ₹1.5L saved. Add 80CCD(1B) NPS for ₹50k more
  • 🏥 Health Insurance — 80D saves up to ₹1L. Cover parents too
  • 🏠 Home Loan — ₹2L interest (24b) + ₹1.5L principal (80C) = ₹3.5L deductions
  • 📱 Leave Encashment — Plan retirement to maximize ₹25L exemption

For Investors

  • 📉 Tax Loss Harvesting — Sell losing stocks before March 31 to offset capital gains. Re-buy after 30 days
  • 📊 ₹1.25L LTCG Free — Book equity profits up to ₹1.25L every year tax-free
  • 🪙 Hold SGBs to Maturity — Zero tax on capital gains. Best gold strategy
  • 📈 Hold Equity > 12 months — LTCG (12.5%) vs STCG (20%). Holding saves 7.5%
  • 🔄 Step-Up SIP Redemption — Redeem oldest SIPs first (FIFO) to maximize LTCG
  • 🏠 Section 54/54F — Reinvest property/asset gains in a house to save LTCG entirely

⚖️ Tax Planning vs Tax Evasion

✅ Tax Planning (Legal)

  • Using 80C, 80D, HRA exemptions
  • Salary restructuring with employer
  • Tax loss harvesting in stock market
  • Choosing Old vs New regime wisely
  • NPS for additional ₹50k deduction

🚫 Tax Evasion (Illegal)

  • Hiding income / taking cash payments
  • Fake rent receipts for HRA claims
  • Inflating business expenses
  • Not reporting foreign assets
  • Penalty: Up to 200% of tax + Jail (3-7 years)
