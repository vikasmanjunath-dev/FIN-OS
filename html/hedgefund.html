EXPECTANCY — The Foundation of All Trading

The single most important number in trading. It answers: "If I execute this exact strategy over 1,000 trades, what is my average profit or loss per trade?" Positive expectancy = statistically inevitable profit. Negative expectancy = statistically inevitable ruin — no matter how lucky you get short-term.

EDGE METRICCATEGORY: FOUNDATIONUSED BY: ALL HEDGE FUNDS
// FORMULA
E = (Win Rate × Avg Win) − (Loss Rate × Avg Loss)Win Rate + Loss Rate = 100% | Example: 40% win rate → 60% loss rate
// WHAT IT ACTUALLY TELLS YOU

A 30% win rate system can be massively profitable if average wins are 5× larger than average losses. An 80% win rate system can slowly bleed you to death if every loss is 10× larger than wins. The win rate alone is meaningless without expectancy. This is the most common misunderstanding in Indian retail trading.

🇮🇳
// RAHUL BHAI'S "LOSING" SYSTEM THAT ACTUALLY PRINTS MONEY

Rahul bhai wins only 4 out of 10 Nifty options trades. His friends mock him constantly — "Tu toh hamesha lose karta hai!"

But: Each win = ₹4,000. Each loss = ₹900.
E = (40% × ₹4,000) − (60% × ₹900) = ₹1,600 − ₹540 = ₹1,060 per trade
100 trades/year → ₹1,06,000 annual edge. His friends with 70% win rates are losing money.

🧠 MORAL: Win rate is vanity. Expectancy is reality. Calculate it before trading any strategy — ever.
// THE HIDDEN TRAP

Expectancy requires a large sample (100+ trades) to be statistically valid. A 20-trade sample can show positive expectancy by pure luck. Many traders backtest 20 trades, declare a profitable system, and blow up their account. Minimum 200 trades for reliable expectancy data.

MAX DRAWDOWN — The Survival Metric

The largest peak-to-trough decline before a new high is reached. Hedge funds treat this as their #1 risk metric because investors don't leave due to low returns — they leave due to pain. A fund that earns 30% per year but drops 60% in the middle will lose all investors during the drawdown, even if it eventually recovers.

RISK METRICCATEGORY: SURVIVALUSED BY: ALL HEDGE FUNDS
// FORMULA
Max DD = (Trough Value − Peak Value) ÷ Peak Value × 100Example: Portfolio goes ₹10L → ₹6.2L → 12L. Max DD = (6.2 − 10) ÷ 10 = −38%
// THE ASYMMETRIC MATH THAT DESTROYS PORTFOLIOS

Recovery math is brutally asymmetric: lose 10% → need 11.1% to recover. Lose 30% → need 42.9%. Lose 50% → need 100%. Lose 70% → need 233%. This is why avoiding large drawdowns is more valuable than chasing large returns.

EXECUTION TOOL

PRE-DECISION CHECKLIST

Your mandatory pre-flight check before every significant financial decision. Pilots use this not because they're forgetful, but because systematic verification catches errors expert intuition misses. Atul Gawande's research: surgical checklists reduced deaths by 47%.

✓
Am I in an emotional state right now?
Fear, FOMO, revenge, excitement — all disable rational analysis. Wait 24 hours minimum if you feel any of these. Non-negotiable.
✓
Have I calculated exact downside in rupees?
Not "the stock could fall" but "my maximum loss on this position is exactly ₹X." If you can't state a specific number — you haven't done homework.
✓
Can I afford to lose this 100%?
Never risk EMI money, medical emergency funds, or children's education. The market is not obligated to time its recovery to your needs.
✓
Does this setup have documented positive expectancy?
Have you backtested 100+ trades of this exact setup? If no historical data exists — it's a hypothesis, not a strategy. Don't risk real money on hypotheses.
✓
Is position size within ¼ Kelly limits?
If this single trade going wrong would affect your monthly lifestyle or sleep — it's too large. Period. No argument.
✓
Have I actively read the bear case?
Find content arguing AGAINST your position. If you can refute their arguments with facts — proceed. If you cannot — reconsider. This defeats confirmation bias systematically.
✓
Will this investment thesis hold for 5 years?
Long-term: is the business fundamentally sound for 5+ years? Trader: does this setup have documented edge over 200+ historical occurrences?
