Debt Protocol

Equity makes you rich. Debt keeps you rich. Stop treating FDs as the only option.

LEVEL 01: NOOB

Lending vs Owning

When you buy a stock, you become an Owner (Risk). When you buy a Bond, you become a Lender (Safety).

🏦 The Indian Reality

Most Indians keep money in Savings Accounts (3%) or FDs (6.5%).

The Hack: Government Bonds (G-Secs) often pay 7.2% - 7.5% with higher safety than a private bank FD. Why let the bank keep the spread?

LEVEL 02: MECHANIC

What is a Bond?

It is a loan you give to the Government or a Corporation.

You give ₹100 Principal
⬇️
They pay ₹7 Interest (Coupon) every year
⬇️
At End: You get ₹100 Back
LEVEL 03: PRO

The Yield Teeter-Totter

This is the hardest concept in finance, explained simply.

If Interest Rates go UP, Bond Prices go DOWN.

📉 Example

You hold an old bond paying 6%. RBI raises rates to 8%.

Nobody wants your 6% bond anymore. To sell it, you must lower the price (Sell at a discount) until the yield matches the new 8% rate.

☠️ GOD MODE: Duration Risk

Long Duration Funds (10 Year+ Bonds): These are highly sensitive to interest rates. If RBI cuts rates, these funds can give 12-15% returns (Capital Gains). If RBI hikes rates, you lose capital.

Strategy: Stick to Liquid Funds or Target Maturity Funds for safety.
