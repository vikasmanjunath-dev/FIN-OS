Equity Protocol

Stop treating the market like a casino. Start treating it like a partnership deed.

LEVEL 01: NOOB

What is a Stock? (The Desi Analogy)

Forget the BSE screen for a second. Let's talk about Raju's Momo Stall.

Raju makes the best momos in Delhi. He sells 100 plates a day. He wants to open a big shop with AC, but he needs ₹10 Lakhs. He only has ₹5 Lakhs.

🤝 The Deal

You give Raju ₹1 Lakh.

In return, Raju gives you a piece of paper that says: "You own 10% of this business forever."

That piece of paper is a STOCK.

If Raju sells 1,000 plates next year, you get 10% of the profit (Dividend). If Raju sells the shop to Haldiram's for ₹1 Crore, your ₹1 Lakh becomes ₹10 Lakhs (Capital Appreciation).

Critical Truth: The Secondary Market

When you buy a share of Tata Motors on Zerodha/Upstox, Tata Motors does NOT get your money.

You are buying it from another person (Uncle Sharma) who wants to sell it. The company only gets money once: during the IPO (Initial Public Offering). After that, it's just people exchanging ownership.

LEVEL 03: PRO

Valuation (The Expensive Shirt Logic)

How do you know if a stock is cheap or expensive? You use the P/E Ratio.

👕 The Shirt Analogy

Imagine a Shirt generates ₹10 of rent per year (Earnings).

  • Case A: Shirt Price is ₹200. (PE = 20). Fair.
  • Case B: Shirt Price is ₹1000. (PE = 100). Expensive!

P/E = Price you pay for ₹1 of Earning.

Market Cap (Size Matters)

Small Cap: Like a Speedboat. Fast, can turn easily, but flips over in a storm.

Large Cap: Like an Oil Tanker (Titanic). Slow, stable, survives storms, but takes 5km to turn.

☠️ GOD MODE: The 1% Knowledge

This is what banks don't teach you.

1. The Free Float Trap

A company has a Market Cap of ₹10,000 Cr. But the Owner (Promoter) holds 90% of shares. Only 10% is available for trading. This is Low Free Float. Operators can easily manipulate the price because there is very little supply.

2. ASM/GSM Lists (The Jail)

If a stock jumps 50% in a week without news, SEBI puts it in the ASM (Additional Surveillance Measure) list. Margins increase to 100%. Speculators get trapped. Never buy a stock in Stage 4 ASM.
