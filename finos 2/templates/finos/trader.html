WHICH TYPE OF TRADER ARE YOU?
UNDERSTAND YOUR STYLE — EACH HAS A DIFFERENT PSYCHOLOGICAL PROFILE AND RISK SIGNATURE
+60 XP
⚡
INTRADAY TRADER
Opens and closes all positions within the same trading day (9:15 AM – 3:30 PM). No overnight positions. Pure technical analysis + tape reading.
Avg Zerodha Users2.3M active intraday/day
Profitable tradersOnly 11% (SEBI 2023)
Primary killerRevenge trading after loss
Most demanding psychologically. Requires: 1) Pre-defined max loss (e.g., max ₹3,000/day), 2) 3-trade rule (stop after 3 losses), 3) No averaging on losing intraday positions. SEBI 2023 study: 89% of intraday traders lose money over 3 years.
BIGGEST ENEMY
After losing ₹5,000 in the morning, most traders try to "recover" by taking bigger positions in the afternoon — this is revenge trading and it's how ₹5,000 losses become ₹25,000 losses.
⚠ Never carry an intraday position to next day "hoping." Square off at 3:20 PM regardless.
🌊
SWING TRADER
Holds positions for 2–20 days. Captures medium-term price moves using charts, technical setups, and market momentum. Less screen time than intraday.
Hold duration2 to 20 trading days
Profitable traders~28% (estimated)
Primary killerBreaking stop-loss on gap-down
Requires overnight emotional control. Your position will gap-down some mornings. The discipline to honour your pre-set stop-loss is the entire game. Works best with NSE stocks having high delivery volume (not penny stocks).
BIGGEST ENEMY
Stock gaps down 6% at open, hitting your stop. Your impulse says "wait, it will recover today." You hold. It falls another 8%. The stop-loss exists precisely for this moment — breaking it once makes it meaningless forever.
⚠ Set stop-loss as a GTT order in Zerodha/Upstox. Never rely on manual discipline alone.
🧨
F&O TRADER
Trades Futures and Options on NSE. High leverage (up to 10–15×). Potentially unlimited gains with options buying, unlimited losses with selling/writing.
NSE F&O turnover₹300+ lakh crore/month
Profitable tradersOnly 7.2% (SEBI 2024)
Primary killerTheta decay + overleverage
SEBI 2024 report: 92.8% of individual F&O traders lost money. Average loss per trader: ₹1.2 lakhs/year. Yet volumes are growing. Why? The illusion of a "big win" once in a while keeps bringing people back. It's structural gambling for most.
BIGGEST ENEMY
Buying 10 lots of NIFTY 24,500 CE at ₹80. It expires worthless. You lost ₹40,000. Next week you buy 20 lots to "recover." This cycle of doubling down is how accounts go to zero in weeks, not years.
⚠ Master paper trading for 90 days before touching real capital. Most people skip this — and pay for it.

F&O SCENARIO
It's Thursday, 3:10 PM — F&O expiry. You sold NIFTY 24,500 CE (call writing) 4 days ago at ₹120 premium. It has decayed to ₹30 — you're sitting on +₹22,500 profit on 50 lots. Then in the last 20 minutes, NIFTY suddenly rallies 180 points. Your short call explodes from ₹30 to ₹220. Your P&L flips from +₹22,500 to −₹37,500 in 12 minutes. 3:20 PM now — 10 minutes to close.
🤔 What do you do?
ASquare off immediately. Accept the ₹37,500 loss and exit cleanly.
BHold — expiry is in 10 min, it might reverse back. You were +₹22.5K.
CBuy more short calls at higher strike to average the position.
DBuy a protective call to hedge the position for last 10 minutes.

March 5–6, 2020 · Stock fell 85% in 2 days · Real event
HISTORICAL CRISIS
March 5, 2020 morning. RBI places Yes Bank under moratorium at midnight. Markets open — Yes Bank opens lower circuit at ₹16.20 (previous close ₹36). You have a swing trade position: 10,000 shares bought at ₹32 avg. Current value: ₹1,62,000 (was ₹3,20,000). The stock is locked in lower circuit — you cannot sell at open. On March 6, it opens at ₹5.55. Your ₹3,20,000 is now worth ₹55,500.
🤔 On March 6 at open (₹5.55), what do you do?
ASell everything at ₹5.55 immediately. Preserve the ₹55,500 remaining.
BHold — RBI will rescue it, too big to fail. It will recover to ₹30+.
CBuy 50,000 more shares at ₹5.55 — massive discount, guaranteed recovery.
DSell 50% now at ₹5.55, hold rest hoping for SBI-led rescue recovery.

THE REVENGE TRADE LOOP — BREAKING THE CYCLE
A typical bad intraday morning · The most common psychological trap
PSYCHOLOGY TRAP
You started the day with ₹50,000 trading capital. By 11 AM you have taken 3 trades — all losses. You're at ₹44,000 (−₹6,000). Your daily max loss was supposed to be ₹5,000 but you "let it run hoping to recover." It's 11:15 AM. You're angry at the market. The voice in your head says: "One big trade and I'll get it all back. NIFTY is clearly going up now." You have 4.25 hours of market left.
🤔 What is the psychologically correct action?
ATake a larger position now — you need to recover the ₹6,000 quickly.
BClose all apps, step away for 30 minutes. No trades until you're calm.
CStop trading for the day. You already exceeded your max loss rule.
DOne more trade — very small size, just to end on a winning note.
